Rock Climbing is the act of scaling stone faces of any height with the use of hands, feet, and sometimes equipment bolted to, hammered in, or temporarily affixed to the rock. This includes the disciplines of bouldering, traditional free climbing, sport climbing, aid climbing, big wall, and even free soloing without any ropes or protection whatsoever! We offer guide service for all of the aforementioned disicplines, for one-day or multiday excursions, and for clients of every skill level from beginner to expert stonemaster!

Bouldering is unroped climbing on shorter cliffs or freestanding boulders. The boulderer generally uses only chalk and climbing shoes to aid his or her ascent, and climbs over foam pads as protection. Bouldering is generally much more difficult than many forms of comparable roped climbing, due to generally shorter climbs and potentially less dramatic falls- though some boulderers climb high enough that they'd suffer serious consequence in the event of a fall!
