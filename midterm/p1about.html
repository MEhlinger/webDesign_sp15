About The Project

This website completed for Spring 2015 Web Development midterm. I chose to do an outdoor guide business.
The first step for creating the site was research. I checked out the websites for some other local guide services, which were, for the most part, pretty simplistic, spartan designs. Many were rather visually busy and poorly layed-out, but some- the simpler- were attractive, and featured many images of mountains, climbs, and the like.

The next step was to lay out the website. I storyboarded the template layout, designing based on the basic "box structure" layout with nested divs. I then outlined the five pages- the mandatory index/home, this about page, and three others, which I determined would be three different outdoor sport disciplines that I had experience with and could knowledgably write about: mountaineering, climbing, and trekking/hiking.

With the website roughly designed, I gathered my images. The background image is a free photo from public-domain-photos.com. The rest of the images were either taken by myself or my friend and climber partner Guille Metzler during our two-month climbing trip last year. I optimized all of those smaller photos with kraken.io's free web image optimization utility, and then shrunk them by percentage to fit nicely in with the text.

Enjoy the site!
